A Tribute To Mohammed Ali "The Greatest"

17 January 1942 - 3 June 2016

Who was Mohammed Ali?

Mohammed Ali born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr was a natural born athlete born on January the 27th in the year 1942 and died on June the 3rd 2016.
Not only was he an exceptional proffessional boxer but he was a philanthropist and an activist too. He believed in helping his fellow coloured brothers
and sisters.Mohammed Ali changed his name from Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr to Mohammed Ali when he converted to Islam, by Malcom X. Mohammed
Ali is also known as The People's Champion as well as The Louisville Lip.

History Of Mohammed Ali - Career

Ali started training as an amateur boxer at age 12. At 18, he won a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the 1960 Summer Olympics and turned proffesionl later that year. He won the heavyweight championship from Sonny Liston at the age of 22 in 1964. Ali was stripped of his heavyweight championship in 1996 due to political reasons. He retired from boxing in 1981 to focus on religion, philanthropinism and activism.

History Of Mohammed Ali - Life

Mohammed Ali was born to Cassius Marcellus Clay Sr and Odessa O'Graddy Clay. Mohammed Ali attended Central High School in Louisville, which is the state he was born in. He suffered from dsylexia, which made it hard for him to read and write much of his life. Apart from boxing Ali was successful as a musician and received two grammy nominations. He also featured as an actor and writer releasing two autobiographies. Ali later in life suffered from Parkinsons disease. He converted to Islam and became a Muslim after 1961, and in 1966 he refused to be drafted into the military which led to him being found guilty of draft evasion so he faced 5 years in prison and was stripped of his title. His conviction was later overturned in 1971.

Mohammed Ali, "The Greatest", shall always be remebered for; his outstanding courage and bravery, his love for his African American brothers and sisters, his boxing and musical talent, and his firm belief in his religion
